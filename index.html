SPACE DOGS

Welcome to the Space Dogs museum website!

During the 1950s and 1960s the Soviet space program used dogs for sub-orbital and orbital space flights to determine whether human spaceflight was feasible. In this period, the Soviet Union launched missions with passenger slots for at least 57 dogs. Most survived; the few that died were lost mostly through technical failures, according to the parameters of the test. [Source]

This website shows the flights that carried dogs over the years and you can see which dogs went together. Click on any flight to know more!
